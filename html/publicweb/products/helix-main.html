Track Results


Use the Build Execution Results to see at a glance how your automated software builds are doing.
  1. Save time and see the status of all builds at once
  2. Drill down into any build from the overview page
  3. Keep as much or as little build history as you want
  4. Separate Production, QA, and Development builds
  5. Annotate builds to track customer patches, hot-fixes




Automatic Notifications


Don't guess about whether the builds are succeeding or failing; let the system notify you automatically.
  1. Provide continuous notification about the health of your source code
  2. Identify failures more quickly, and be proactive in keeping your code working
  3. Notify groups, users, or external mailing lists
  4. Use failure notifications to automatically log bug reports in the system




Share Your Builds


Use Zed to share your builds throughout their lifecycle, through development, QA, and on to production. When someone needs a build, there is always just one place to go: Zed.
  1. Use Zed as your system of record
  2. QA has their own sand-box to pin builds they are reviewing
  3. Production and QA builds keep full details so anyone can review how a build worked, even if it was done last year.
  4. Display custom HTML output from your build steps. Static analysis, testing results, code coverage reports, all visible from the same place
